3.4.4 Pragmatism in Education
“Pragmatism is a temper of mind, an attitude, it is also a theory of the nature of ideas and truth, and finally it is a theory about reality.” -William James.
Pragmatism (व्यावहारिक) as a school of philosophy

Meaning of Pragmatism

Etymologically the word pragmatism is derived from the Greek Word “pragma’ which means activity or the work done. Some other scholars think that the word pragmatism has been derived from the Greek Word ‘Pragmatikons’ which means practicability or utility. Thus according to this ideology great importance is laid upon practicability and utility. Pragmatists firmly hold that first the activity or experiment is done and then on the basis of results, principles or ideas are derived. Hence, pragmatism is also known as experimentalism or consequentialism. It is called Experimentalism because Pragmatists believe experiment as the only criterion of Truth. To them Truth, Reality, Goodness or Badness are all relative terms. These concepts are not predetermined and absolute. They are proved by man’s own experiences.
Further, Pragmatists believe that truths are many and they are all in the making. Man researches these areas only by means of his own experiments and experiences. Hence, only those things are true which can be verified by experiments. Pragmatists also hold that whatever was true yesterday. Pragmatists also hold that whatever was true yesterday; need not be the same today. Under these circumstances no definite and determined principle of current use can stop the world from moving forward on the path of progress.
Pragmatism is called consequentialism because any human activity is evaluated in terms of its consequences or results. If the activity results in some utility, then it is true otherwise not. It may be noted that the fundamental start of pragmatism is ‘change’.
In this sense no truth I is absolute and permanent. It is always changing from time to time, from place to place and from circumstances to circumstances. Thus those ideas and values which are useful in one set of circumstances, times and places, need not prove to be the same in changed circumstances, place and times. Hence Pragmatists, unlike the Idealists, do not believe in fixed, eternal and absolute values of life to be followed in all times, places and circumstances. They also do not uphold any predetermined philosophy of life. To them, only those ideas and values are true which result in some utility to mankind in a certain set of circumstances, places and times.
Fundamental principle/Characteristics of pragmatism

The following are the Philosophical premises of pragmatism in brief as well

  1. Truth is manmade
  2. Truth is always changable
  3. Reality is in the process of making
  4. man power is the supreme power
  5. Spiritual elements have no significancein our behaviour
  6. There is no final or fixed (absolute) values of life.
  7. Action is real, ideas are tool or instruments
  8. Problems are the motives of truth
  9. It is pluralistic philosophy
  10. The world is incomplete and indeterminate
  11. Man exists with the continuation of the world
  12. It supports to theory of utility
  13. man is a social animal

Pragmatism and educative process
This ideology does not uphold any outdated, lifeless and rigidly traditional method of teaching. Pragmatists believe that minds of different children are different. Hence a certain fixed method of teaching cannot be useful to all. For the purpose of developing children on sound lines, both the child and the teacher should together think and formulate the necessary and useful method of teaching which is related to the interests of the child and involves practical work, activities and productive experiences. According to which teaching methods may be devised and formulated on the following points:

  1. To emphasize the child centered educative process.
  2. Process of integration or correlation.
  3. Emphasis on learning by doing process. Pragmatists give more importance to action rather than ideas. Pragmatism attaches more importance to activity and experience in preference to thoughts or ideas. According to this ideology education should be imparted through activities and practical experiences.
  4. Emphasis on Project method that was formulated a solid and practical method of teaching by Kilpatrick who is the ardent disciple (students) of John Dewey. Through this method, the child learns by his own activities and experiences, the teacher only guiding and suggesting wherever and whenever there is any necessity for such help. According to him, “A project is whole hearted purposeful activity proceeding in a social environment.” Similarly, According to Ballard, “A project is a bit of real life that has been imparted into the school.”
  5. Analysis of useful and useless things through experiment and practice.
there are some methods which are appropriate to use in different situations.
such are illustrated as follows:
  1. Project method
  2. Methods based on principle of Child Centered
  3. Discussion method
  4. Educational tour
  5. Library study
  6. Laboratory Methods
  7. Learning by doing Methods
  8. Observation method
  9. Correlation and Integration method Etc.

Pragmatism and role of teacher
According to pragmatism, the teacher works as a friend, philosopher and guide to the children. As such, he needs two qualities namely-

1. Capacity to come into close contacts of children to know their interests and
2. Full understanding regarding the conditions and situations of changing society. According to this ideology, the teacher is expected to put before children such problems corresponding to their interests that they get busy in solving the problem and gain more and more knowledge by their own efforts. At the same time the teacher should also create such situations that all children are able to develop social interests, attitudes and habits for the welfare of whole society of which they are integral parts. According to pragmatism, the major roles of teachers are as follows:

  1. Teacher as a important person
  2. Teacher as a guide and helper
  3. Teacher as a creator of new ideas
  4. Teacher as a creator or learning environment according to capacity and necessity of the child
  5. A person who respects the freedom of learner
  6. A person who works as a member of students group.
  7. Teacher as a evaluator of capacities and needs of learners.
  8. Teacher as a planner of education
  9. As a person of scientific tendency.

Pragmatism and role of Student
There are some points, which are the major role of students

  1. Pragmatists accept the child centered education.
  2. Student is the centre of learning and them emphasis acquired experiences. And self experiences that solved the causes of problems.
  3. Students may lay down on the learning by doing environment and stimulate learning by self activity.
  4. Students try to creation of new things
  5. To develop knowledge and experience on the basis of utility and their significance.
  6. Pragmatism advocates the merging of play with work
  7. They will develop self-confidence, self-reliance, co-opreation, sympathy and fellow-felling for others.
  8. To develop a sense of social responsibility and becomes a true citizen in the real sense of the term.
  9. They will develop a sense of social discipline and moral obligation towards self and others.
  10. Students should develop values for him.
